# Plural nouns (-s) and countability

Countable nouns need a plural -s when there is more than one. Korean marks
plurals optionally (들), so the English -s is frequently dropped.

- we are friend → we are **friends**
- I have two brother → I have two **brothers**
- many people, three cups, both hands (always plural form)

Noun must agree with its determiner:
- "these book" → "these **books**"
- "one of my friend" → "one of my **friends**" (after "one of" → plural)

Uncountable nouns take no -s: information, advice, homework, furniture.
- "many informations" → "much **information**"

Skill vs skills, experience vs experiences:
- "my speaking skill" → "my speaking **skills**" (a set of abilities).

Drill idea: find every countable noun and check it has the right number.
