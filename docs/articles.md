# Articles (a / an / the) and zero article

Korean has no articles, so learners both omit them and overuse "the".

Rules of thumb:
- First mention of a countable singular noun → **a/an**: "I watched **a** movie."
- Already known / specific / unique → **the**: "**The** movie was great." / "**the** moon".
- Proper nouns (movie titles, most country/person names) take **no article**:
  - "the Interstellar" → just "**Interstellar**"
  - "the Korea" → just "**Korea**"
- Plural / uncountable in general sense → **no article**: "I like **movies**",
  "**Water** is essential."

Common learner errors:
- Dropping a/an: "I have car" → "I have **a** car".
- Adding *the* to a title or general plural: "I enjoy **the** movies" (general) → "I enjoy movies".
- "some part" vs "**some parts**" / "**part of it**" — countable nouns need number agreement.

Drill idea: label each noun in a paragraph as a / the / zero and justify.
