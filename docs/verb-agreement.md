# Subject–verb agreement (3rd person singular -s)

In the present simple, a third-person singular subject (he, she, it, a single
person or thing) takes a verb ending in **-s / -es**.

- he go → **he goes**
- she like → **she likes**
- it mean → **it means**
- my brother watch movies → my brother **watches** movies

Why Korean learners drop it: Korean verbs do not conjugate for person or number,
so the -s feels redundant and is easily omitted in fast speech.

Common traps:
- Words like *everyone, everybody, each, the news* are singular → take -s:
  "Everyone **knows**", "The news **is** surprising".
- Two singular nouns joined by *and* are plural → no -s: "Tom and Jane **like**…".
- The -s only appears in the **present**. Past tense is the same for all subjects:
  "he **went**", not "he goes-ed".

Drill idea: rewrite 5 sentences about a friend using "He/She + verb-s".
