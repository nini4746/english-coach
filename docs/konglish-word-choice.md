# Konglish and unnatural word choice

Direct translation from Korean often produces grammatically possible but
unnatural English. Prefer natural collocations and varied vocabulary.

Overused vague words → upgrade:
- "so much fun" (repeated) → "really enjoyable", "a lot of fun", "fascinating"
- "so good / so nice" → "excellent", "impressive", "delicious" (be specific)
- "kinda deep" → "quite profound", "thought-provoking"

Common Konglish / L1 interference:
- "I have no time" is fine, but "time poverty" (시간 빈곤) is not idiomatic → "I'm short on time".
- "fighting!" (응원) → "good luck!", "you can do it!"
- "meeting" used for a date → "I have a date".
- "skinship" → "physical affection".

Verb–noun collocations:
- "do a mistake" → "**make** a mistake"
- "take a rest" is OK; "do a rest" is not.

Drill idea: list 3 words you repeat, write 3 natural synonyms for each.
