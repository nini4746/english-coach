# Tense: present perfect vs simple present/past

The **present perfect** (have/has + past participle) links the past to now. It is
used for duration up to the present and for experiences without a specific time.

Duration with *for / since* → present perfect, NOT simple present:
- we know each other for ten years → we **have known** each other for ten years
- I live here since 2020 → I **have lived** here since 2020

Stative verbs (know, like, be, have) with a time span almost always need the
present perfect. Korean has no exact equivalent tense, so learners default to the
present, which sounds wrong to native ears.

Past participle traps:
- "it mean that love is connect" → "it means that love **is connected**"
  (passive needs the participle *connected*, not the base *connect*).

Simple past vs present perfect:
- Finished, specific time → simple past: "I **watched** it yesterday."
- Unspecified / up-to-now → present perfect: "I **have watched** it before."

Drill idea: practice "I have + p.p. + for/since …" frames.
